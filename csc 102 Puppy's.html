Development Stages

Neonatal Period (0-2 weeks) Physical: Eyes and ears are closed, and puppies rely on their mother for warmth and nourishment. Behavior: Mostly sleep and eat, with little interaction

Transitional Period (2-4 weeks)

Physical: Eyes and ears open, begin to explore their surroundings. Behavior: Start to walk and play with siblings, begin social interactions.

Socialization Period (4-12 weeks)

Physical: Coordination improves, and they are more active. Behavior: Crucial time for socialization. Expose them to various people, pets, and environments to develop a well-adjusted adult dog.

Juvenile Period (3-6 months)

Physical: Rapid growth, teething. Behavior: Increased independence, may test boundaries.

Adolescence (6-18 months)

Physical: Near adult size. Behavior: May display stubbornness or increased energy. Training and consistency are important.
